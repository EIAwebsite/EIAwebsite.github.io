OUR MISSION

Western Engineers In Action (WEIA) aims to provide isolated communities with safe access to services and opportunities by building footbridges over impassable rivers. Working under our parent organization, EIA, students are connected to real world civil engineering projects that create positive impact. Students get the opportunity to perform design work and structural calculations, use AutoCAD, calculate material quantities and costs, create construction Gantt charts and write professional reports. Since 2017, WEIA has partnered with 9 universities to build 7 bridges in 2 countries. These projects have helped over 15,800 people in underserved communities gain year-round access to water, health care, education, jobs and markets. As a university chapter of EIA, our team at Western University strives to fund, design, and construct a new footbridge every year for a community in one of our project countries, Bolivia and Eswatini. We aim to create a global impact, changing lives for the better of the locals living in these isolated communities while developing engineering skills and immersing ourselves in new cultures.

For more information about our parent organization, visit: www.eiabridges.org




WHY BRIDGES?

In the remote farmlands of the developing world, walking is the primary mode of transportation. The World Bank estimates that a billion people in low-income countries around the world lack access to a road that is passable year-round, largely due to rainy seasons that are both critical to agriculture and dangerous for the very people that rely on those seasonal rains. When rivers swell, walks to school or work or visits to a doctor can become life-threatening without a bridge to cross. Studies from rural communities throughout the world have demonstrated that access to safe and reliable transportation networks has a dramatic effect on the ability of residents to meet their own needs and care for their families.
Footbridges result in...

12% increase in children enrolled in school
18% increase in healthcare treatment
45% increase in fertilizer investment
56% increase in crop yield
75% increase in farm profits
30% increase in labor market income
59% increase of women in the labor market
20% return on investment

This level of impact is unparalleled in the realm of community-level development projects! Building on training received from Bridges to Prosperity, the EIA Bridge Program uses a replicable model that leverages the resources and capacity of local governments and the innovative design and construction technologies developed in partnership with top engineers to create transformative change in access to education, health services, markets, and employment for hundreds, or even thousands, of people at a time. Our model is focused on pairing innovative bridge-building technology with the resources and capacity already present in a community to create lasting, positive change, all while empowering our student participants to become lifetime global change-makers.
